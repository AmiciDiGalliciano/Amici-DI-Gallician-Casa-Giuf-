Practical advice before arrival

Arrive prepared, experience a better stay

Casa Giufà is located in the village of Gallicianò: an authentic, quiet place, far from services always at hand. A minimum of organization before coming up makes the stay simpler and more peaceful.

01

Stop at the supermarket first

Before arriving in Gallicianò, we recommend purchasing water, snacks, personal products, any medicines, and what might be needed for children, the elderly, or guests with special needs.

02

Communicate allergies and needs to Elisa

In case of allergies, food intolerances, specific requests, or special needs, notify Elisa before arrival. She will be able to collect useful information and communicate it to Trattoria Nizio Paleò, when necessary.

03

Bring what makes you feel good

A sweatshirt for the evening, comfortable shoes, a water bottle, and small personal products help to better experience the village, the trails, and the slow pace of Gallicianò.

Simple goal: allow every guest to have a good stay, without useless surprises and with the right information before departure.

Time changes pace here

You don't come here to look at a screen

Casa Giufà does not offer Wi-Fi and TV. It's not a hidden flaw: it's a precise choice. Fewer distractions, less noise, more silence, more sleep, more dialogue, and more attention to what Gallicianò makes you feel.

When the brain stops jumping between notifications and continuous stimuli, it begins to notice the place again: the scent of the earth, the sound of footsteps, the fiumara, the light on the stones, the simple flavors, and human time.

Who we are

Giuseppe: Your Local Host

Giuseppe Zindato was born right in this house, when Gallicianò was lived with hands, steps, and senses: the stable under the house, the animals, the well water, the scents of cheeses, the fiumara and the mill reached as a boy to bathe.

After a life built in Reggio Calabria as an entrepreneur in the fruit trade and a football coach, Giuseppe recovered his father's house to give back to guests an authentic piece of his Gallicianò: nature, memory, simplicity, and silence.

The Territory

Grecanic Calabria

"Here Greece is not a distant memory. It has remained in the names, in the stones, in the paths, and in the language itself of the people."

Gallicianò, Bova, Amendolea, Condofuri: places where the ancient Grecanic language still resonates among the stone houses and the fiumara. A protected, isolated, and preserved area. One of the last pockets of Magna Graecia to survive through the centuries.
